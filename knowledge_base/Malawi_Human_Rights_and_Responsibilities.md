# Page 1: Malawi Human Rights and Responsibilities

## Key Concepts
- Human rights in Malawi are protected by the Constitution and international conventions.
- Every citizen has the right to life, dignity, education, health, and equal protection.
- Responsibilities include respecting the rights of others and following national laws.
- Citizens must participate in national development and uphold peaceful coexistence.
- Children’s rights include protection from abuse, access to education, and safe environments.

## Additional Details
- The Malawi Human Rights Commission monitors violations and ensures compliance.
- Freedom of expression must be exercised without promoting hate or violence.
- Responsibilities include maintaining public safety and respecting community structures.
- Citizens are expected to report corruption, abuse, and human rights violations.
- Equal treatment applies regardless of gender, tribe, disability, or economic status.

- Human rights in Malawi are protected by the Constitution and international conventions.
- Every citizen has the right to life, dignity, education, health, and equal protection.
- Responsibilities include respecting the rights of others and following national laws.
- Citizens must participate in national development and uphold peaceful coexistence.
- Children’s rights include protection from abuse, access to education, and safe environments.

## Additional Details
- The Malawi Human Rights Commission monitors violations and ensures compliance.
- Freedom of expression must be exercised without promoting hate or violence.
- Responsibilities include maintaining public safety and respecting community structures.
- Citizens are expected to report corruption, abuse, and human rights violations.
- Equal treatment applies regardless of gender, tribe, disability, or economic status.
