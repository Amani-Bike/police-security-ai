### Q: What should I do if I'm being followed?
**A:** 
1. Change direction and cross the street
2. Head toward populated, well-lit areas
3. Do not go home - lead them away from your residence
4. Enter a store, restaurant, or police station
5. Call 911 and provide your location and description of follower
6. Use your phone to take photos/video if safe to do so

### Q: How can I secure my home against burglary?
**A:**
**Physical Security:**
- Install deadbolt locks on all exterior doors
- Use security cameras at entry points
- Install motion-activated lighting
- Keep shrubs trimmed near windows
- Use window locks and security film

**Behavioral Security:**
- Don't advertise absences on social media
- Use timers for lights when away
- Keep valuables out of sight from windows
- Get to know your neighbors for mutual watch

### Q: What's the safest way to walk at night?
**A:**
- Stay in well-lit, populated areas
- Walk confidently and aware of surroundings
- Avoid using headphones that limit awareness
- Have your keys ready before reaching your destination
- Vary your routes and times
- Use campus security escort services if available

## üöó Vehicle Security

### Q: What should I do during a traffic stop?
**A:**
1. **Safe Stopping:**
   - Turn on hazard lights to acknowledge officer
   - Find well-lit, populated area if possible
   - Pull over to right shoulder completely

2. **Interaction Protocol:**
   - Keep hands visible on steering wheel
   - Wait for officer's instructions
   - Inform officer before reaching for documents
   - Be polite and cooperative

3. **Your Rights:**
   - You have the right to remain silent
   - You can refuse consent to search your vehicle
   - You have the right to ask if you're free to leave

### Q: How can I prevent car theft?
**A:**
- Always lock doors and close windows
- Never leave keys in vehicle
- Park in attended, well-lit lots
- Use steering wheel lock or alarm system
- Don't leave valuables visible in car
- Consider GPS tracking device for high-risk areas

## üíª Digital Security

### Q: How do I protect against identity theft?
**A:**
- Shred documents with personal information
- Use strong, unique passwords for accounts
- Enable two-factor authentication
- Monitor bank and credit statements regularly
- Freeze credit reports when not needed
- Be cautious of phishing emails and calls

### Q: What should I do if my wallet is stolen?
**A:**
**Immediate Actions (First 2 hours):**
1. Call credit card companies to cancel cards
2. File police report for documentation
3. Place fraud alert on credit reports

**Within 24 Hours:**
- Contact major credit bureaus
- Notify bank about stolen checks
- Replace driver's license and IDs
- Change online banking passwords

## üë• Workplace Security

### Q: How should I handle suspicious packages?
**A:**
**Warning Signs:**
- Unexpected or unfamiliar sender
- Excessive postage or packaging
- Strange odors or stains
- Wires or electronic components visible

**Response Protocol:**
1. Do not open, shake, or examine
2. Isolate the package immediately
3. Evacuate the area
4. Call 911 and security
5. Wash hands if you handled the package

### Q: What's the protocol for active shooter situations?
**A:**
**RUN (First Option):**
- Have an escape route and plan in mind
- Leave your belongings behind
- Keep your hands visible
- Help others escape if possible

**HIDE (If Escape Not Possible):**
- Lock and barricade doors
- Silence mobile devices
- Hide behind large objects
- Remain quiet and calm

**FIGHT (Last Resort):**
- Act with physical aggression
- Improvise weapons
- Commit to your actions
- Work together with others

## üö∏ Child Safety

### Q: How do I teach children about stranger danger?
**A:**
- Teach them to never go with strangers
- Establish safe family code words
- Identify safe adults (police, teachers, security)
- Practice "what if" scenarios
- Teach them to trust their instincts
- Use the "No, Go, Yell, Tell" method

### Q: What should children do if they get lost?
**A:**
- Stay in one place - don't wander
- Look for a police officer or security guard
- Find a mother with children
- Go to cashier or information desk in stores
- Never leave the area with anyone except safe adults
