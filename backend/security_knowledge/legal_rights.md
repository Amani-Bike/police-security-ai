## üèõÔ∏è Constitutional Rights

### Fourth Amendment - Search and Seizure
**Your Rights:**
- Protection against unreasonable searches and seizures
- Generally requires warrant for home searches
- Vehicle searches have different standards
- Right to refuse consent to search

**What You Can Say:**
- "I do not consent to this search."
- "Am I free to leave?"
- "I choose to remain silent."
- "I want to speak with a lawyer."

### Fifth Amendment - Self-Incrimination
**Your Rights:**
- Right to remain silent
- Protection against self-incrimination
- Right to due process
- Protection against double jeopardy

**Practical Application:**
- You can remain silent during police questioning
- You must explicitly state you're invoking this right
- Silence alone may not be enough in some states
- Always identify yourself when required by law

### Sixth Amendment - Legal Representation
**Your Rights:**
- Right to legal counsel
- Right to speedy and public trial
- Right to confront witnesses
- Right to be informed of charges

**When It Applies:**
- Once formal charges are filed
- During police questioning after arrest
- At all critical stages of prosecution

## üëÆ Police Interactions

### During Traffic Stops
**Your Responsibilities:**
- Provide driver's license
- Provide vehicle registration
- Provide proof of insurance
- Comply with lawful orders

**Your Rights:**
- Right to ask why you were stopped
- Right to refuse consent to search vehicle
- Right to remain silent beyond identification
- Right to record the interaction (check local laws)

### During Arrest
**What to Do:**
- Remain calm and compliant
- Clearly state: "I am exercising my right to remain silent"
- Clearly state: "I want to speak with a lawyer"
- Do not resist physically
- Remember badge numbers and details

**What Not to Do:**
- Do not discuss your case with anyone but your lawyer
- Do not make statements or confessions
- Do not consent to any searches
- Do not become argumentative

## üè† Home Rights

### Police at Your Door
**Without Warrant:**
- You are not required to open the door
- You can speak through the door or window
- Ask "Do you have a warrant?"
- If no warrant, you can refuse entry

**With Warrant:**
- Ask to see the warrant
- Verify the address is correct
- Check what areas the warrant covers
- Do not interfere with the search
- Contact your lawyer immediately

### Emergency Exception
Police can enter without warrant if:
- They hear sounds of violence or distress
- They see evidence of crime in plain view
- They are in hot pursuit of suspect
- There is imminent danger to life

## üó£Ô∏è Recording Rights

### Audio Recording Laws
**One-Party Consent States:**
- You can record conversations you're part of
- No need to inform other parties
- Check your state's specific laws

**All-Party Consent States:**
- All participants must consent to recording
- Illegal to record without everyone's knowledge
- Includes phone conversations and in-person talks

### Video Recording in Public
**General Rule:**
- You can record police in public spaces
- Keep safe distance and don't interfere
- Right to record public officials performing duties
- Some restrictions on audio recording may apply

## üë• Witness Rights

### If You Witness a Crime
**Your Rights:**
- Right to remain safe during observation
- Right to refuse to speak with anyone but police
- Right to have lawyer present during questioning
- Right to protection if feeling threatened

**Your Responsibilities:**
- Provide truthful information to police
- Cooperate with lawful investigation
- Appear in court if subpoenaed
- Preserve any evidence in your possession

### Witness Protection
**When Available:**
- If your testimony places you in danger
- For serious criminal cases
- Requires prosecutor's approval
- Involves relocation and identity changes

## üåê Digital Rights

### Phone and Digital Privacy
**Your Rights:**
- Generally need warrant for phone searches
- Can refuse to provide phone passwords
- Right to privacy in digital communications
- Protection against unreasonable surveillance

**Exceptions:**
- Border searches may have different rules
- Employer-owned devices may have limited privacy
- Emergency situations may allow access

### Social Media and Online Activity
**Legal Protections:**
- Right to anonymous speech in most contexts
- Protection against unlawful monitoring
- Rights vary by platform terms of service
- Law enforcement needs warrant for private messages
